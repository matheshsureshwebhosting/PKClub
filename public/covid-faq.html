Covid Frequently Asked Questions

1What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

Fever, cough, Tiredness, Sneezing, Running nose, Muscle Aches, sore throat, Headache, Chills, Nausea, Vomiting, Loss of taste, Loss of smell, body pain, diarrhoea, abdominal pain. 

2 Whom to approach if I develop COVID-19 symptoms?/ Where to go if I have symptoms?

If you develop symptoms, you can approach the nearest Govt./Corporation health facilities/fever camps and also you can consult the doctors through the GCC VIDMED app.

3Do I need to test for covid 19?

If you have covid 19 symptoms like fever, loss of taste, loss of smell, tiredness, body pain, breathlessness then you have to do covid 19 test in near by centers.

4 What should I do if I have COVID-19 symptoms?

If you have any symptoms of COVID-19, call your healthcare provider or COVID-19   helpline for instructions and find out when and where to get a test. Stay at home till you get a test result and be away from others. If you have shortness of breath seek medical attention at a health facility immediately. 

5 What test should I take if I have symptoms?

You can do RT-PCR tests for COVID-19. 

6 How long will it take to get the test result? 

Generally, 24-48 hours.

7 If the SWAB test is negative, can I still have COVID-19?

Yes. If you have breathlessness and the SWAB test is negative, you can take a CT scan, to confirm COVID-19.

8 If I tested positive, can I choose whether I should be in a hospital, COVID-19 Care Centre (CCC) or home isolation?

If you positive go to nearby screening centers and the doctor in the screening centre after seeing your reports will decide whether you should be in home isolation or CCC or Covid Hospital.

9 Who will provide medicine and necessary essentials if I’m positive?

Doctors in the screening centers/CCC/CH will decide about your medications. GCC Focus volunteers will facilitate the essential things needed.

10 Should other family members also need to be tested?

Yes, the other family members should also be tested if you are covid positive.

11 Whether I’m at a higher risk if I’m pregnant and COVID-19 positive?

Depends upon the severity of the infection, should consult your obsetrician.

12 What effect will coronavirus have on my baby if I’m diagnosed with the virus?

It wont affect your baby, but you should consult your obsterician

13 What can I do to protect myself?

Wear a proper face mask, maintain physical distance, wash hands regularly, and avoid gathering.

14 Which pre-existing medical conditions put me at higher risk for developing severe symptoms for coronavirus?

Diabetes, Pulmonary Tuberculosis and Lung cancer.

15 After quarantine can I test again? 

No need to do test.

16 Which is the best vaccine?

Both have undergone research, and both are effective.

17 What are the symptoms after vaccination?

Fever, body pain, local tenderness at injection site and tiredness.

18 What is the exact time interval for Covishield  second dose?

COVISHIELD - 6-8 weeks after the first dose.

19 What is the exact time interval for covaxin second dose?

COVAXIN - 4 weeks after the first dose.

20 How to get doctor consultation via online?

You can consult a doctor by installing the GCC VIDMED app.

21 Shall I get COVID-19 before taking my second dose of Vaccine?

Yes you can get positive after taking the first dose but the infection will be less. 

22 Can I take one dose of Covaxin and another dose of Covishield?

No, you should take same vaccine for first and second dose.  

23 Can I donate blood after vaccination?

Yes, 28 days after the second dose. 

24 How to rectify mistakes in vaccination certificates?

You can approach the vaccine center or Zonal office. 

25 Can I travel to another state or another country?

Yes, you can travel to another state or another country with RTPCR negative certificate (48-72 Hours) and E-pass. 

26 Should I need to test before and after travelling?

Yes. 

27 Should I undergo RTPCR if I’m Traveling?

Yes. 

28 What are the do’s after vaccination?

You can do your daily routine activities.

29 Can I choose which COVID-19 vaccine?

Yes you can choose but in any case of severe allergic problem or severe illness consult your family doctor for choosing the vaccine. 

30 How long does covid 19 vaccine protection lasts?

There are limited evidence available. However, experts told the vaccine is effective for the period of one year.  

31 Do we need to pay money for vaccination?

Vaccinations are free of cost in all government hospitals, UPHC and UCHC. In private hospitals government fixed some price for vaccine.

32 Can pregnant or Lactating people be vaccinated?

No. 

33 After vaccination can we take non- veg ?

There is no diet restriction following vaccination.

34 After vaccination can I take alcohol or smoke?

No. The effect of vaccination will reduce if you intake alcohol or if you smoke. 

35 Can I take medicine for the side effects after I get Vaccine?

Yes, you can consult your family doctor. 

36 Should I stop my current medication during quarantine?

No, you should continue your medications as advised by your family doctor. 

37 If I have tested negative, will I get a negative certificate?

Yes, you can get your test results from where you have tested.

38 Who should I contact to get a negative certificate?

You can contact the sanitary inspector / Zonal health officer.

39 What if the second dose is delayed due to some reason?

You can take a second dose any day after 4 to 6 weeks for COVAXIN and 6-8 weeks for COVISHIELD.

40 First dose was taken overseas (Covishield equivalent). Can a second dose be taken in India?

Yes. That is possible. If the same options are available.

41 Cancer patient can take vaccine?

Consult your oncologist and get vaccinated.

42 Do I need to take a second dose at the same place where I took the first dose?

Yes, but you can also take second dose in other centres if vaccine is not available in that centre.
